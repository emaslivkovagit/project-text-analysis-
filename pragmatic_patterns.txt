Basic sentiment analysis
------------------------
Method: Asent and spacytextblob sentiment scores.
Features: negative sentiment, neutral sentiment, positive sentiment, compound sentiment, polarity, and subjectivity.
Observation: Stories had an average negative sentiment score of 0.054, while non-stories had an average score of 0.052. Stories had an average compound sentiment score of 0.070, while non-stories had an average score of 0.085. Stories had an average subjectivity score of 0.476, while non-stories had an average score of 0.492.
Rule: Basic sentiment scores can be used as supporting evidence, but not as the main rule. If one label has clearly stronger negative, positive, compound, or subjective language, this may give weak evidence for that label.
Explanation: Sentiment analysis is useful for detecting attitudes and opinions, but story and non-story texts can both contain emotional or subjective language. Therefore, sentiment alone is not expected to be a strong distinction.

Pattern 1: Emotional language
-----------------------------
Method: Count emotion words per 100 words.
Story average: 0.194
Non-story average: 0.146
Rule: If emotion_per_100 is higher than approximately 0.170, this gives evidence for story.
Explanation: This pattern may help because stories often describe personal or emotional experiences. However, it can fail because non-stories on Reddit can also contain strong opinions, complaints, or emotional reactions.

Pattern 2: Temporal discourse markers
-------------------------------------
Method: Count temporal markers per 100 words, such as then, after, when, later, suddenly, eventually, and finally.
Story average: 1.646
Non-story average: 0.824
Rule: If temporal_per_100 is higher than approximately 1.235, this gives evidence for story.
Explanation: This pattern is useful because stories often organize events in a time sequence. It can fail when non-stories explain steps, processes, or examples in chronological order.

Pattern 3: Personal stance markers
----------------------------------
Method: Count expressions per 100 words, such as I think, I feel, I believe, personally, honestly, for me, and to me.
Story average: 0.403
Non-story average: 0.417
Rule: If stance_per_100 is lower than approximately 0.410, this gives evidence for story.
Explanation: This pattern may help when stories are written as personal experiences. However, it can fail because non-stories can also include personal opinions or arguments.

Pattern 4: Sentiment shift
--------------------------
Method: Split each text into beginning, middle, and end. Then calculate the difference between the highest and lowest compound sentiment score.
Story average: 0.331
Non-story average: 0.391
Rule: If sentiment_shift is lower than approximately 0.361, this gives evidence for story.
Explanation: This pattern may help because stories can develop emotionally from beginning to end. It can fail when a story has a stable emotional tone or when a non-story contains a strong change in opinion.

Conclusion
----------
The basic sentiment scores were useful as a starting point, but they do not fully capture the difference between stories and non-stories. More useful pragmatic patterns come from discourse-level features: emotional language, temporal organization, personal stance, and sentiment development across the text. These features focus on how language is used to express attitudes, personal involvement, and narrative structure.

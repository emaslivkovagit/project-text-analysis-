Basic sentiment analysis
------------------------
Method: Asent and spacytextblob sentiment analysis.
Observation from development data:
Stories had an average negative sentiment score of 0.054. Non-stories had an average negative sentiment score of 0.052.
Stories had an average neutral sentiment score of 0.558. Non-stories had an average neutral sentiment score of 0.562.
Stories had an average positive sentiment score of 0.079. Non-stories had an average positive sentiment score of 0.082.
Stories had an average compound sentiment score of 0.070. Non-stories had an average compound sentiment score of 0.085.
Stories had an average subjectivity score of 0.476. Non-stories had an average subjectivity score of 0.492.
Rule: Sentiment scores were used as supporting pragmatic information, but not as the main prediction rule.
Explanation: Sentiment is useful for pragmatics because it shows emotion, attitude, and subjectivity. However, sentiment alone does not clearly separate stories from non-stories.

Pattern 1: Emotional language
-----------------------------
Method: Count emotion words per 100 words.
Story average in development data: 0.194
Non-story average in development data: 0.146
Rule: Emotional language was used as a supporting pattern, but it was not used for the final prediction.
Explanation: This pattern may work because stories often describe personal or emotional experiences. It can fail because non-stories can also contain emotional opinions or complaints.

Pattern 2: Temporal discourse markers
-------------------------------------
Method: Count temporal markers such as then, after, when, later, and finally.
Story average in development data: 1.646
Non-story average in development data: 0.824
Rule applied to test data: If temporal_per_100 is higher than 1.235, predict story. Otherwise, predict non-story.
Test accuracy: 0.742
Explanation: This pattern works well because stories often describe events in a time sequence. It can fail when non-stories explain steps or processes in order.

Pattern 3: Sentiment shift
--------------------------
Method: Split the text into beginning, middle, and end, then measure the change in sentiment.
Story average in development data: 0.331
Non-story average in development data: 0.391
Rule: Sentiment shift was used as a supporting pattern, but it was not used for the final prediction.
Explanation: This pattern may work because stories can develop emotionally over time. It can fail when a story has a stable tone or when a non-story changes tone strongly.

Final prediction method
-----------------------
The final prediction uses one simple pragmatic rule: temporal discourse markers. The development data showed that stories had more temporal markers than non-stories. Therefore, the system calculated the middle point between the story average and the non-story average. Test texts above this threshold were predicted as story. Test texts below this threshold were predicted as non-story.
Final accuracy on the test data: 0.742

Conclusion
----------
The most useful pragmatic pattern was temporal discourse markers, because stories often describe events in chronological order. Emotional language and sentiment shift were included as supporting pragmatic observations, but they were not used for the final prediction. Basic sentiment analysis was also included because it is relevant to pragmatics, but it did not clearly separate stories from non-stories.
